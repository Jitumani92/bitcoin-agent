Bitcoin developer Paul Sztorc announces BTC hard fork called eCash

📰 Cointelegraph.com News 📅 April 25, 2026 Original Source →

Bitcoin developer Paul Sztorc, known for his work on the decentralized prediction market platform, has announced a new project that involves a hard fork of the Bitcoin blockchain. The new coin will be named eCash. According to Sztorc's announcement, the hard fork will introduce a new, competing layer-1 blockchain and seven layer-2 scaling networks designed to enhance Bitcoin's scalability and privacy features.

This development matters for Bitcoin as it signifies continued efforts to improve the cryptocurrency's infrastructure and address some of its longstanding issues, such as slow transaction speeds and limited privacy. The introduction of a new layer-1 blockchain could potentially ease congestion on the main network, while the seven layer-2 scaling networks aim to increase transaction capacity without compromising decentralization.

For investors, this hard fork presents both opportunities and risks. While the potential improvements in scalability and privacy offered by eCash could attract users away from Bitcoin, there is also a chance that the market may view this as a contentious split, potentially leading to short-term volatility. It's crucial for investors to thoroughly research and understand the implications before making any decisions regarding their Bitcoin holdings.

💡 Key Takeaway

The announcement of eCash, a hard fork of Bitcoin led by developer Paul Sztorc, promises to introduce new scaling solutions and potentially enhance privacy features, but it could also lead to short-term market volatility for Bitcoin investors.
