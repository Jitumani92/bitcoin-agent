Bitcoin analysts explain why BTC price can’t take out $80K

📰 Cointelegraph.com News 📅 May 01, 2026 Original Source →

In recent days, the price of Bitcoin has struggled to surpass the $80,000 mark. According to several Bitcoin analysts, there are three key factors that are preventing the cryptocurrency from making a significant breakthrough. Firstly, a large overhead supply cluster is putting pressure on the price, making it difficult for BTC to sustain any substantial upward momentum. This supply cluster represents a significant amount of sell orders at higher prices, which are acting as a resistance level and preventing Bitcoin from advancing.

Secondly, increased profit-taking activity among investors is also contributing to the current price action. As the price of Bitcoin approaches its all-time high, some investors who purchased the cryptocurrency at lower prices may be taking profits, thereby adding selling pressure and capping the upward move. This is a common phenomenon in financial markets when an asset reaches a significant milestone or achieves a substantial increase in value.

Finally, the resumption of spot Bitcoin ETF outflows is another factor that analysts believe is keeping the price pinned below $80,000. Some Bitcoin exchange-traded funds (ETFs) have recently seen an outflow of assets, which means that investors are withdrawing their holdings from these funds rather than buying more. This outflow could be indicative of a bearish sentiment among institutional investors and further strengthen the resistance at higher prices.

💡 Key Takeaway

The ongoing resistance at $80,000 is being driven by a large overhead supply cluster, increased profit-taking activity, and the resumption of spot Bitcoin ETF outflows, making it difficult for the price to break through this level and potentially setting up a short-term bearish scenario.

While this projection is highly speculative and relies on numerous assumptions, it offers a tantalizing glimpse into what might be possible for the digital currency in the coming years.
