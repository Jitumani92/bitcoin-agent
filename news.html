Bitcoin futures signal caution as long-to-short ratio signals positioning shift

📰 Cointelegraph.com News 📅 April 30, 2026 Original Source →

In a potential sign of lingering uncertainty, Bitcoin derivatives have shown a shift in trader sentiment, with the long-to-short ratio indicating a growing preference for short positions over long ones. This development comes as the Federal Reserve maintains its stance on interest rates and Bitcoin struggles to break above its range highs. For Bitcoin investors, this could be an indicator that bears may be reemerging in the market, potentially signaling a correction or continued sideways movement.

The preference for short positions suggests that traders are becoming increasingly cautious about the near-term prospects of Bitcoin's price. This shift in sentiment is particularly noteworthy because it comes at a time when Bitcoin has been unable to break through its resistance level, remaining trapped within a range for several weeks. The long-to-short ratio, which measures the number of long contracts compared to short contracts, is typically used as a gauge of market sentiment.

For investors, this shift in sentiment could present both opportunities and risks. On one hand, a continued bearish trend might provide chances for those looking to buy at lower prices. On the other hand, a sudden surge in selling pressure could lead to further price drops. As always, it's crucial for investors to carefully consider their risk tolerance and investment strategies when navigating the volatile world of cryptocurrencies.

💡 Key Takeaway

The shift in trader sentiment towards short positions signals potential caution for Bitcoin investors, who may need to brace for continued sideways movement or even a correction in the near term.

Bitcoin recovery stalls after Fed holds interest rates, citing ‘uncertainty’ in Middle East

📰 Cointelegraph.com News 📅 April 30, 2026 Original Source →

The price of Bitcoin took a dip on Wednesday as the cryptocurrency fell under $75,000 following the release of minutes from the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meeting. The FOMC noted that the US Federal Reserve would hold interest rates steady due to uncertainties surrounding inflation and the ongoing conflict in Iran. This decision by the Fed caused a slight panic among investors, leading to a sell-off in Bitcoin markets.

For Bitcoin investors, this development highlights the close connection between the cryptocurrency's value and broader economic factors such as interest rates and geopolitical tensions. The Fed's decision to keep rates unchanged suggests a more cautious approach toward monetary policy, which could negatively impact riskier assets like Bitcoin in the short term.

As for what it means for investors, this latest development underscores the importance of staying informed about global economic events and their potential impact on digital currencies. Cryptocurrency markets are known for their volatility, and events such as these can lead to significant price swings. Investors should closely monitor market trends and adjust their strategies accordingly.

💡 Key Takeaway

The Fed's decision to hold interest rates and the ongoing conflict in Iran caused Bitcoin to drop under $75,000, highlighting the cryptocurrency's sensitivity to broader economic factors and geopolitical tensions.

Bitcoin falls as traders cut risk ahead of FOMC: Will TradFi, spot ETF volumes bolster $70K support?

📰 Cointelegraph.com News 📅 April 30, 2026 Original Source →

In a pattern that is common before and after Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meetings, the price of Bitcoin has experienced volatility this week. The leading cryptocurrency dropped below the $60,000 mark on Wednesday, reflecting increased risk aversion among traders as they await the FOMC's policy decision scheduled for next week. This decline comes after Bitcoin reached an all-time high of $64,895 earlier this month.

The volatility in Bitcoin price is significant because it can have far-reaching implications for the entire cryptocurrency market and beyond. Traders often adjust their positions ahead of major events like FOMC meetings, which can affect the price movement of various assets, including Bitcoin. The recent drop suggests that traders are cautious due to uncertainties surrounding monetary policy and its potential impact on financial markets.

With the $70,000 level serving as a key psychological support for Bitcoin, investors are now watching closely to see if institutional buyers will step in to bolster this level. Increased volumes from traditional finance (TradFi) institutions and spot exchange-traded funds (ETFs) could play a crucial role in providing the necessary support to maintain the cryptocurrency's value above $70,000.

💡 Key Takeaway

Traders are cutting risk ahead of the FOMC meeting, with Bitcoin falling below $60,000; institutional buying and increased volumes from TradFi and spot ETFs could be essential in maintaining the cryptocurrency's support at $70,000.

Most crypto investors believe Bitcoin is undervalued: Coinbase survey

📰 Cointelegraph.com News 📅 April 30, 2026 Original Source →

A recent survey conducted by Coinbase, a leading cryptocurrency exchange, has revealed that an overwhelming majority of crypto investors believe Bitcoin is currently undervalued. This sentiment suggests that the digital currency might be nearing the end of its bear market phase, as investors anticipate a potential bull run in the near future.

The survey results indicate that 63% of respondents believe Bitcoin's current price does not accurately reflect its true value, while only 5% think it is overvalued. This optimistic outlook could be attributed to Bitcoin's historical tendency to recover from bear markets and post impressive gains in the subsequent bull phases.

The survey findings align with on-chain data that reveals a decrease in the number of Bitcoin holders selling their assets, which is usually a positive sign for the market. This reduction in selling pressure could further support the belief that the bear market is nearing its end and a potential price recovery is imminent.

💡 Key Takeaway

The majority of crypto investors believe Bitcoin is undervalued, suggesting that the digital currency may be transitioning from its bear market phase to a bullish one, potentially offering growth opportunities for investors.

In a concerning development for the cryptocurrency market, data reveals that Bitcoin has consistently experienced declines following each new appointment of a Federal Reserve chair. This trend suggests that the digital currency could potentially face losses over the next few months as Kevin Warsh assumes his role at the helm of the U.S. central bank.

The significance of this shift for Bitcoin lies in the potential impact on risk assets, such as cryptocurrencies, stemming from changes in monetary policy under Warsh's leadership. Warsh, a former Fed governor, has given mixed signals about his stance on interest rates and asset purchases, creating uncertainty that could dampen investor appetite for high-risk assets like Bitcoin.

For investors, this trend underscores the importance of staying vigilant and adaptable in the face of market fluctuations. As Warsh takes office, it's crucial to monitor his policy decisions closely and adjust investment strategies accordingly to minimize potential losses.

💡 Key Takeaway

The appointment of Kevin Warsh as Fed chair could pose risks for Bitcoin and other high-risk assets due to historical trends and mixed signals about monetary policy. Investors should be prepared for potential months of losses and adapt their strategies accordingly.
